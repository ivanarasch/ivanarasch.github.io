About

Building at the physical-digital boundary

I'm a music technologist and studio engineer whose work spans three registers: the tactile world of PCB design and firmware, the acoustic world of recording and mixing, and the mathematical world of deep learning for musical expression.

I hold an M.S. in Music Technology from NYU Steinhardt and a dual B.S. in Computer Science and Audio Technology from American University. Currently, I work as a studio engineer at the Clive Davis Institute of Recorded Music.

My long-standing interest is how embodied controllers shape the way humans interact with generative systems — and what new forms of musical agency become possible when you design both the hardware and the model.

Selected work
Thesis / Hardware + ML

DJing in Latent Space

A custom MIDI DJ controller (Teensy 4.1, KiCad PCBs, AS5600 encoders, VL53L0X proximity sensors, Trill Bar capacitive strip) that interfaces via Web MIDI to a browser-based MusicVAE interpolation engine. Physical gestures control real-time navigation through a 512-dimensional latent space, with output routed to a Yamaha Disklavier acoustic piano. Includes a live 11-step spherical interpolation system and within-song attribute vector computation for density and interval control.

Teensy 4.1 KiCad MusicVAE Web MIDI Web Audio API Magenta Disklavier
ML / Spatial Audio

Sound Event Detection + DOA Estimation

Multi-task SELD model for 4-channel First Order Ambisonics audio. Computed STFT features across all channels, estimated DOA in 3D Cartesian coordinates, and built a combined SED+DOA architecture based on DCASE 2019 Task 3.

Python FOA STFT DCASE PyTorch
Audio ML

Audio Style Transfer Research

Systematic evaluation of neural audio style transfer architectures for real-time use in live performance: Magenta RealTime, AFTER, RAVE, and DDSP. Benchmarked latency, timbral control, and playability as criteria, ultimately mapping the constraints of each approach before pivoting to the MIDI domain.

RAVE DDSP AFTER Magenta
